Working in Progress

Identifying Economies of Scope for Multiproduct Firms

Draft available upon request

Abstract: Estimating economies of scope is fundamental to understanding firm behavior and market structure, yet it is empirically challenging when firms exercise market power and input sharing across product lines is unobservable. Because economies of scope are defined by cost subadditivity in a joint technology, measuring them requires a framework that explicitly models joint production. This paper proposes a structural framework for identifying and estimating economies of scope in multiproduct firms by exploiting the duality between production and cost functions. The approach identifies the multiproduct production frontier and the associated implicit cost structure without requiring observing product-specific input allocations, thereby delivering a theory-consistent scope measure while avoiding the conceptual and data limitations of standard cost-function approaches. We establish nonparametric identification for the production frontier under regularity conditions. Monte Carlo experiments demonstrate that the estimator recovers the true structural parameters. We demonstrate an application to linked French administrative and production microdata and show how the recovered cost structure can be used for merger and breakup counterfactuals.
